This is the doxygen documentation for the exercises.

  1. basicI/O (about: ifstream, string, stringstream, using input operators & testing stream states)
  2. sizes (about: sizeof & "padding")
  3. relations (about: member initialisation & constructors, enum, methods, pre-condities & testing )
        HINT: Take a look at the unittest example before designing your own testcases.
  4. cage (about: containers & iterators)
  5. operators (about: header files, operators & friends)
  6. employees (about: inheritance, virtual methods & polymorphism)
  7. finally (about: singleton & exceptions)

Usefull websites:

  • http://en.cppreference.com/w/Main_Page
    - very up-to-date
    - gives very precise and sometimes very formal information
    - can be intimidating for novices
  • https://isocpp.org/wiki/faq/
    - the language C++ and how to use it sensibly plus explanations of it
  • http://www.cplusplus.com/reference/
    - simple reference about various libraries
    - novice friendly, but omits information/facts
  • And if you search the internet for "quick solutions" (rather than trying to figure out the answers yourself) most people on stackoverflow.com usually have some sensible ideas.
